What is a Cellular Automaton?

A cellular automaton (CA) is an abstract model of computation consists of a grid of tiles. Each tile consists of a state, usually represented as a color. The state is determined by its neighboring tiles which may differ from CA to CA.

What we have here is a special case of CA--the elementary CA. Popularized by Stephen Wolfram, ECAs are one-dimensional CAs. Despite its simplicity, ECAs (and CAs in general) display complex behaviors.

Play with this minimal sandbox and discover the world of CAs! Instructions are in the README file.
